BOOTH SET-UP AND GUIDELINES

Every booth should have the following:

  • A large size flag (for hanging on wall above your booth), the name of the country, and a sign that says "Hello" in the language of your country.
  • The boards should be standard tri-fold presentation boards and the content should be appropriate for elementary-level educational purposes.
  • The answer to your passport question should be clearly displayed on your board.
  • The tables (upon which the boards will sit) should have a table cloth.
  • Do not bring items that are fragile or too valuable since children like to touch the displays.
  • Only use blue painter's tape for hanging things on walls per school regulations.
  • ALL booths and tables must be removed immediately following the evening event.

    • **You can begin setting up your booth on Monday, March 2nd after school, or Tuesday morning the 3rd before class visitations begin.

PASSPORT

  • A week prior to the fair, the students will receive a "passport" where every country being represented will be featured along with a question.
  • Please come up with a question about your country RELATED TO THE THEME OF MUSIC and submit by February 7.
  • The answer to the passport question should be easily found on the display board.
  • Every country has space for a unique stamp, or you can choose to sign it with your initials or use small stickers.
  • Stamps and ink to be provided by each country booth presenting.

    • **The stamp should only be used on the passport, not the children.

EVENING PERFORMANCES 6:10-6:45pm

  • Performance sign-ups are due by February 1.
  • Performances MUST be cultural and representative of a specific country.
  • Performances must be by children of Lydiksen Elementary only.
  • Performances are limited to no more than 2 minutes in length ~ PLEASE BE MINDFUL OF THIS!
  • The Following details must be emailed to us no later than February 14.
    • Special music requirements (i.e piano needed, etc)
    • Name of Country/Cultural Heritage of Performance
    • Type of performance (song, dance, demonstration, etc.)
    • Names of child/children participating
    • One sentence description of the performance
  • Sound System in MPR: Music can be played directly from your smartphone if you have a 3.5mm headphone jack. If you have a newer smartphone/iphone which does not have this jack, then you will need an adaptor. For apple phones, a lightning to 3.5mm headphone Jack adaptor costs $9.00 and can be purchased via amazon or the apple store.
  • ALL performances will take place on the raised stage in the MPR at Lydiksen with appropriate stage lighting.
  • Group performances will be given priority over individual performances. The international Fair team also will use their own discretion in order to create a fair and balanced number of performances with representation from a diverse set of countries when possible.

FOOD

  • You are welcome and encouraged to bring food samples for the evening open house event.
  • NO FOOD ALLOWED DURING SCHOOL HOURS
  • Bite size finger food sampling is great, but please try to minimize paper and utensil waste.
  • Please bring a note with the name of the dish in English and in your native language.
  • Please provide a list of the ingredients for those visitors who may have allergies. Keep in mind this is just a "sample".

**We only serve the food AFTER the performances, so please keep it out of sight until the performances have completed.

FASHION SHOW

  • Only children of Lydiksen and their young siblings can participate in the fashion show.
  • The fashion show will begin promptly at 6:00pm
  • Please sign up your child no later than February 14th.
  • The children will need to gather outside in front of the MPR beginnings at 5:30pm
  • We will arrange the children by country in Alphabetical order.
  • Everyone participating from a specific country will enter the stage together, representing that country.
  • Their first names are announced as they walk onstage
  • They will need to hold a Small Flag (on a stick) of their country [most all small country flags can be purchased on Amazon)
  • They will need to wear an appropriate cultural outfit from their country.
  • They will enter the stage on one side, stop & wave, and exit the stage on the opposite side. We will have music playing during this time.
  • Once all the participants have passed onstage, they will all gather and, together, sing "Its a Small World". Here is the youtube link: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7jiaU0xbOKs
